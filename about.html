Philosophy

The Pianist. The Researcher.

[此处开始你的 Pianist 叙事] Music has never been merely a sequence of notes to me; it is an architectural structure built in time. From the earliest moments of my piano studies, I was captivated not just by the emotional resonance of the repertoire, but by the profound logic underlying it...

My time at the Tianjin Juilliard Pre-College shaped my understanding of the stage. Every performance is a delicate balance of vulnerability and precision. Whether I am navigating the complex counterpoint of Bach or the expansive narratives of Chopin, my goal is to reveal the hidden geometries of the composer's mind.

[此处自然过渡到 Researcher] But the questions I asked at the keyboard inevitably led me away from it. Why does a specific harmonic progression evoke a universal human response? Can the intuition of phrasing be quantified? This pursuit brought me to Denison University, where I study Applied Mathematics alongside Musical Arts.

As a researcher, I am deeply invested in the intersection of Machine Learning and Music Information Retrieval (MIR). I view mathematical theorems and algorithms not as cold tools, but as a different language for understanding the same beauty I experience on stage. To me, the piano and the code editor are two instruments playing the same symphony.
